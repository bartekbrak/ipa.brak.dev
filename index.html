## Programmer's [IPA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet) This page will help you learn some IPA in context of programming jargon. version alpha: confusing, works but has little content * go right to see the words. * Hover and `CTRL` + Hover to hear the words and individual sounds. * `Esc` - overview. * go down for project notes
* For simplicity I will use [Received Pronunciation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Received_Pronunciation), the one I think I use, If there's interest in [General American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_American) we'll supply that in the future. Sorry for the imperial bias. * For audio, I will source wiktionary where I can, and forvo where I can't find the pronunciation * All individual sounds come from Wikipedia
#### Various links for those who want to go deeper: * [Wikipedia - IPA pulmonic consonant chart with audio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPA_pulmonic_consonant_chart_with_audio) * [Wikipedia - IPA vowels chart with audio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPA_vowels_chart_with_audio) * [Wikipedia Help:IPA for English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English) * [Interactive IPA Chart - University of Victoria](http://web.uvic.ca/ling/resources/ipa/charts/IPAlab/IPAlab.htm) * [Interactive IPA Chart by Jonathan Dowse](http://jbdowse.com/ipa) * [Phonetic resources - Jennifer Smith, UNC-Chapel Hill](http://www.unc.edu/~jlsmith/pht-url.html#%298%29) - lots of phonetics software, way more than you need
